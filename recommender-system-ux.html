The User Experience of Recommender Systems

We are designing methods to understand the usefulness and usability of algorithmically generated reading and editing recommendations.

Project overview

The primary focus of this research is to develop methods for understanding the user experience of algorithmically generated recommendations. English Wikipedia alone contains more than 5 million articles, with new articles being added every day. With so many articles to choose from, it can be difficult for a Wikipedia reader to answer the question What do I want to read next? Similarly, with so many articles that need improvement, it can be difficult for editors to answer the question What do I want to edit next?

Over the past few years, the Wikimedia Foundation has begun to develop software features that attempt to answer these questions by building algorithmic models that automatically suggest articles for people to read, edit, translate, or create based on other people’s reading and editing behavior and on characteristics of the articles themselves.The overall goal of these new features is to make reading and editing more engaging by helping users filter out “noise” (i.e., articles they are not interested in right now) and focus on the articles that are most relevant to their current interest, context, or activity.

Building these features and the models they are based on involves a series of important design decisions and often tradeoffs. There are many ways to generate recommendations and just as many ways to display them. However, like all software projects, the end goal is always to answer the question How well does this feature support the defined task for the intended audience in their anticipated context?
